Introduction to Brush Lettering

By Nealy Kehres

Scroll through the images below for a step-by-step guide to hand lettering the word "Happy" in a trendy, modern calligraphic style with a high-quality brush pen and paper or iPad Pro and Apple Pencil. You'll never buy a greeting card again once you master hand lettering!

After learning how to write "Happy," you'll be ready to write phrases like the one above.

The arrows direct you which movement your pen should make. When stroking downward, gradually apply more pressure to your pen. When stroking upward, take off the pressure.

Unlike regular cursive, you can take your pen off the paper while brush lettering. Oftentimes, letters require it. Add the two seperate strokes as shown to create a single letter.

The unusual loop on top of the "p" gives the word a festive and unique vibe. While making large swooping loops like this, moving your hand quickly and naturally will result in the most desirable look. You can repeat the loop on the second "p" or do a simple down stroke as I did.

The "y" also requires a swooping loop. With practice, it will come naturally to you.
